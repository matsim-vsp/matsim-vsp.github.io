## Project description

VSP is part of the [Collaborative Research Center SFB/TRR 408 "AgiMo: Data-driven agile planning for responsible mobility"](https://gepris.dfg.de/gepris/projekt/531327426) with the two projects. 

The transportation sector has been an unquestioned driving force of societal progress for decades. However, it is now 
facing unprecedented challenges, e.g., rapid decarbonization, broader demands for equity, and livable cities. 
Efficiency gains from technological innovation tend to be offset by unintended consequences such as increasing traffic volumes. 
Integrated planning approaches are key to address these challenges but current research methods in planning are often fragmented, 
and too focused on motorized transportation and efficiency gains. The opportunities from new data sources are not fully considered, 
and models are often too technical in their implementation to be suitable for evidence-based participatory planning methods. 
Phase I of the proposed CRC will address the identified research gaps with the following four main research goals:
- develop a new set of consistent scientific mobility planning methods, including behavioral data and models, and methods for planning and managing transportation systems and mobility services, for all modes from walking to motorized vehicles, while taking advantage of new data sources;
- integrate a new set of modular metrics for responsible mobility, considering what we call the ``4F principles'' with the four dimensions Function, Form, Fairness, Forever;
- embed the new planning methods into the open data AgiMo Digital Twin that provides up-to-date network-wide assimilated mobility data and the models for generating this data with fast turnaround;
- develop participatory planning methods based on the technical outcomes from the AgiMo Digital Twin to create future scenarios for responsible mobility that are technically well-grounded and at the same time represent stakeholder preferences.

The long-term perspective for this CRC is to further develop the AgiMo Digital Twin in its components and their integration, its functionalities and its interaction with an active community into a virtual laboratory that enables research and supports practical planning tasks.

### Project C3 - Integration of data and models towards the AgiMo Digital Twin

Transport models typically need a lot of time and effort (several person months) to be set up. This
is true even if a model already exists and only needs to be updated to more recent data. Also,
models are normally built for a “typical day”, meaning that they do not follow different demand levels
over the seasons, during school vacations, etc. This means that giving model-based answers to
transport-related policy questions typically takes at least a couple of months.
In this project, we will address these issues by moving towards the open data AgiMo Digital
Twin of the real world transportation system. The aspiration is a digital twin for planning purposes,
implying that the time scale of the system reaction is several months, and the feedback loop into
the real system is not a technical channel, but by feeding the model results into the political process, 
in this CRC via C4 to C2. The starting point will be a simulation of a region, in combination
with a setup that updates (‘assimilates’) that simulation to additional data regularly, initially once
per week. The results will be uploaded to the data infrastructure of INF, leading to automatically
downloadable assimilated data. The ultimate goal will be to have such a system track real world
developments, e.g., differences between weekdays, reduced traffic during school holidays, reactions to street closures or special events, etc.
We will leverage a data feed previously utilized in the context of COVID-19-related simulations,
which provides daily out-of-home durations at zipcode level (cf. INF for the data procurement) –
this will be used to increase or decrease activity participation levels accordingly. Another data
feed, with much smaller sample size but much better spatial resolution, will provide changes in
activity participation by activity type, thus allowing to adjust not just the overall participation levels,
but to do this by activity type. Methods from C7 will be used to give the data assimilation process
a theoretical foundation. Work in A6 will be tightly integrated to more realistically model the active
modes walking and cycling.
Projects such as B2, B7, C5, and C6 will use the automatically uploaded data via INF as input
to their own work. Besides the “digital twin” research agenda, this project thus serves as an
umbrella project for many of the data and modeling projects in this CRC (such as B3 or A7)
and as a use case for stress-testing the data and modeling techniques developed in INF.
The main research question of this project is: 
- Which data items and methodologies need to be combined, and in which way, to build a credible planning digital twin of the real world transportation system?

### Project C4 - Simulation-based development of coherent scenarios for decarbonized transportation systems and responsible mobility

This project is the modeling companion project to C2. C2 will, via novel methods of interaction with
stakeholders and possibly using input from C6, first discuss and and suggest possible desirable
futures, and then discuss and suggest pathways to these desirable futures. This project will im-
plement these pathways into models and quantitatively investigate if they reach their stated goals
while complying to accepted constraints, such as carbon limits. If the pathways are not coherent
with goals and constraints, this project will develop methods to come up with alternatives that do
so, and feed them back into the process in C2. We call this approach simulation-based back-
casting, since it will use the simulation to develop coherent alternatives. With coherent we mean
that emergent outcomes, such as desired modal splits, are generated from concrete policy mea-
sures through credible behavioral mechanisms, and we mean that possible interactions between
different policy measures are correctly represented. This project will also integrate the metrics of
C1. The following two research questions are formulated for C4:
- How can a simulation-based approach support the societal search for possible futures that
include decarbonization and responsible mobility?
- Which possible pathways to those futures can be identified in feedback loops between tech-
nical simulation and participatory planning?
